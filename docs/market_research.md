# FirstClub Market Research Snapshot

Research snapshot: May 2026.

This memo uses public sources, app-store evidence, and the observed app journey. It is designed to make the project credible as a product-growth strategy artifact without pretending to know FirstClub's internal metrics.

## 1. Category Context

India quick commerce is large and still expanding. IBEF, citing CareEdge, reported that quick-commerce gross order value reached Rs. 64,000 crore in FY25 and is projected to reach Rs. 2,00,000 crore by FY28. The same note says quick commerce still serves only around 1% of India's grocery demand.

Strategic implication: FirstClub does not need to copy the speed war. There is enough category headroom for a differentiated quality wedge.

Source: https://www.ibef.org/news/quick-commerce-orders-soar-to-rs-64-000-crore-us-7-47-billion-in-fy25-to-touch-rs-2-00-000-crore-us-23-34-billion-by-fy28

## 2. FirstClub Positioning

FirstClub's public positioning emphasizes:

- Quality-first groceries.
- 200+ harmful ingredients left out.
- Tested, verified, and curated products.
- A "ClubHouse" idea rather than an anonymous dark-store promise.
- A broad grocery surface: fruits and vegetables, dairy, breads, staples, kids food, packaged foods, snacks, supplements, cleaning, pooja, and more.

Strategic implication: The app should not feel like a generic quick-commerce grid. It should make curation and proof visible across launch, browse, cart, delivery, and recovery.

- https://www.firstclub.site/banned-ingredients

## 3. Reported Business Signals

Moneycontrol reported in March 2026 that FirstClub was in talks to raise USD 50-60 million at around a USD 250 million valuation. The same report said FirstClub had:

- Delivered over 1.2 million orders.
- Sold over 15 million units.
- Served more than 2 lakh customers.
- Reached roughly Rs. 1,500 AOV.
- Grown around 45% compounded monthly over recent quarters.
- Planned expansion to 35 stores within six months.
- Planned category additions including home, kids food, pet care, and gifting.
- Experimented with subscriptions and cafe formats.

Earlier reporting also positioned FirstClub around top-income households willing to pay a premium for clean-label, high-quality products. TechCrunch reported operating signals including a curated SKU base, strong repeat behavior, 60% exclusive products, and a customer base skewing toward women and higher-income households.

Strategic implication: FirstClub is no longer only proving that a quality-led quick-commerce wedge can attract trial. At 1M+ order scale, the product-growth system must preserve trust while scaling repeat, recovery, category width, and city expansion.

## 4. App Store And Android Signals

Apple's App Store listing shows a high rating base and recent version updates around performance, experience, product ratings, recommendations, reordering, and commonly purchased together. Public review snippets show a useful split:

Positive themes:

- Clean-label and high-quality products.
- Curated selection instead of overwhelming catalog depth.
- Smooth app experience for some users.
- Fresh/organic grocery appeal.

Negative themes:

- Missing or wrong items.
- Slow support resolution.
- Subscription or wallet confusion.
- Discovery/search friction.
- Price/value skepticism.
- Quality inconsistency in some orders.

AppBrain lists FirstClub on Android with roughly 360k total downloads, 100,000+ Play downloads, and a 4.33 rating from around 3k ratings.

Strategic implication: The top of funnel has enough signal to justify deeper activation, review-risk, support, and lifecycle experiments. The review pattern is especially important because FirstClub's trust promise raises the cost of every operational defect.

## 5. Consumer Need

PwC India's Voice of the Consumer 2025 reported that 84% of Indian consumers see food safety as a vital driver. It also reported meaningful use of digital delivery platforms and continued concern about food costs.

Strategic implication: FirstClub must do two jobs at once:

- Make the quality proof specific and credible.
- Make the premium feel worth it when users compare prices with Blinkit, Instamart, Zepto, BigBasket, Amazon, or offline stores.

Source: https://www.pwc.in/press-releases/2025/84-of-indian-consumers-seek-safer-healthier-food-choices-pwc-report.html

## 6. Strategic Read

FirstClub's opportunity is to build the operating system for premium trust:

- Trust before checkout through proof, personalization, and Hero SKU samples.
- Trust during fulfillment through picker verification, substitution discipline, and ClubHouse transparency.
- Trust after defects through instant, high-empathy recovery.
- Trust over time through category-width CRM, subscription transparency, and post-aha referrals.

The project therefore focuses on qualified I2FO, category-width retention, recovery loops, and city playbook repeatability instead of a generic discount-first growth plan.
